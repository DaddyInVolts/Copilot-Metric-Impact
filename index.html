Section 1

Problem & Opportunity

Problem Statement

M365 Copilot leadership needs to understand which experiments are moving their key business metrics — both positively and negatively. Today, there is no centralized view that answers the question: "Which experiments improved or degraded a specific metric?" Leaders must manually cross-reference individual experiment scorecards, consult with data scientists, or rely on ad-hoc queries to piece together the metric-level impact of experiments across the portfolio. This process is time-consuming, error-prone, and makes it difficult to identify who to recognize for metric wins or who to engage for deeper investigation on metric regressions.

Opportunity

The Expedite platform already houses all of the underlying data — experiment scorecards, metric results, statistical significance, and experiment metadata. By surfacing a dedicated "Metric Impact" view, we can give leadership an instant, filterable answer to "what moved my metric?" This enables faster decision-making, clearer accountability, and a tighter feedback loop between experimentation results and business outcomes. The feature naturally complements the existing Copilot Experiment Impact page by flipping the lens from experiment-centric to metric-centric.

Evidence

  • Leadership Need: CVPs and GMs regularly ask "what experiments moved [metric X]?" — a question that currently requires manual data assembly by PMs or data scientists
  • Existing Infra: The Copilot Experiment Impact page already aggregates experiment data with filters; this feature extends that foundation with a metric-first perspective
  • Accountability Gap: Without a single view, positive experiment results go unrecognized and negative results go unaddressed, delaying corrective action
Section 2

Goals & Success Metrics

Goals

  1. Enable leadership and experiment owners to instantly see which experiments improved or degraded a selected Copilot metric
  2. Rank experiments by impact magnitude so the biggest movers are immediately visible
  3. Provide drill-through links so users can navigate directly to experiment detail pages for deeper investigation
  4. Ensure statistical rigor by excluding experiments that do not meet the p ≤ 0.05 significance threshold

Section 3

User Scenarios

Scenario 1 — "The Accountability-Driven CVP" (Primary)

Jessica is a CVP overseeing M365 Copilot. During her weekly business review, she wants to know which experiments moved "Chat Seen WAU (100%)" in the last 30 days. She navigates to the Copilot Metric Impact page from the left nav, selects "Chat Seen WAU (100%)" from the metric dropdown, filters to "production" ring and "Commercial" audience, and immediately sees a ranked bar chart. The top experiment added +427K users and is green; at the bottom, one experiment shows a -12K regression in red. She clicks the top experiment name and is taken directly to its detail page, where she can see the full scorecard and identify the PM to recognize at her next staff meeting.

Scenario 2 — "The PM Investigating a Metric Dip"

Raj is a PM who noticed that "Retained WAU (100%)" dropped this month. He opens the Metric Impact page, selects the metric, and scans the chart for red (negative) bars. He identifies three experiments with statistically significant negative impact. He opens the detail table below the chart to compare their start dates, products, and impact magnitudes. He clicks "Details" on the largest regressor to view its full scorecard and begins a root-cause investigation, armed with the exact experiment name, feature, and progression details.

Edge Case — Duplicate Experiments

An experiment named "Copilot Chat Redesign" appears under the same Feature name, Progression name, and Product in multiple experiment steps with varying traffic volumes. The page automatically deduplicates by selecting only the experiment step with the greatest treatment count, ensuring the chart is not cluttered with redundant entries and the impact shown reflects the most representative data point.

Edge Case — No Significant Experiments

A user selects "DSAT Ratio" with a narrow date range and finds that no experiments meet the p ≤ 0.05 threshold. The page displays an empty state message: "No statistically significant experiments found for this metric with the selected filters. Try broadening your date range or adjusting filters." The chart area shows a placeholder rather than an empty or misleading visualization.

Page Layout

  • Top Bar: Filter controls in a horizontal row — Start Date, End Date, Status, Ring, Audience, Product — followed by the Metric selector dropdown. "Copy Link" and "Export to Excel" buttons anchored to the right.
  • Chart Area: A horizontal or vertical bar chart ranking experiments from most improved to most degraded. Bars are colored green (positive impact) and red (negative impact). The Y-axis dynamically scales to the data range. Hovering over a bar shows a tooltip with experiment name, impact value, and p-value.
  • Table Area: A data table below the chart matching the Copilot Experiment Impact table format. Columns include: IDEAS Recommendation (Ship/No-Ship badge), Name, Status (Active/Stopped badge), Product, Started date, Ring, Audience, and the selected metric value. Each row has a "Details" link.

Key Interaction Notes

  • Changing the metric dropdown immediately refreshes both the chart and the table — no separate "Apply" button needed.
  • Filter changes also trigger an immediate refresh of both views.
  • The chart and table always show the same set of experiments — they are two views of the same filtered, deduplicated, significance-gated dataset.
  • Table columns are sortable. The default sort order matches the chart: descending by metric impact value.

Edge Cases & Empty States

  • No significant experiments: Both chart and table areas display: "No statistically significant experiments found for this metric with the selected filters. Try broadening your date range or adjusting filters."
  • Single experiment: Chart renders with one bar; table shows one row. The Y-axis still scales to the data point range.
  • Large dataset (50+ experiments): Chart remains readable with scrolling or pagination. Table supports pagination or infinite scroll to handle large result sets.
  • Outlier experiments: If one experiment's impact is orders of magnitude larger than the rest, the dynamic Y-axis may compress smaller bars. Consider adding a tooltip or annotation to ensure smaller-impact experiments remain visible.

Accessibility

  • All filter controls, the metric dropdown, and table sort headers are keyboard-navigable via Tab / Shift+Tab and activatable with Enter or Space.
  • Chart data is accessible via a screen reader — each bar announces: "[Experiment Name], impact: [value], p-value: [value]."
  • Color is not the sole indicator of positive/negative impact — values are also signed (+/-) in both tooltips and the table.
Section 6

Open Questions & Risks

Open Questions

Q1: Should the metric list be hardcoded or dynamically populated from available scorecard metrics?

V1 ships with a fixed list of 7 Copilot metrics. Future versions may need a dynamic or admin-configurable metric list as the Copilot metric portfolio expands. Owner: David Lydston (PM). Current leaning: Hardcoded for V1, with a plan to make it configurable in V2.

Q2: What is the default metric selection when the page first loads?

Should the page default to a specific metric (e.g., "Chat Seen WAU (100%)"), or require the user to make a selection before any data renders? Owner: David Lydston (PM).

Q3: How should the chart handle extreme outliers?

If one experiment's metric impact is a large multiple of the next-highest experiment (e.g., +400K vs. +40K), the dynamic Y-axis may compress smaller bars to near-invisible widths. Should the chart use a log scale, a broken axis, or a tooltip-based approach for these cases? Owner: David Lydston (PM) + Expedite Engineering.

Risks

Risk 1: Outlier Distortion in Chart Visualization

Likelihood: Medium · Impact: Medium. Experiments with the largest and smallest metric movements may be incorrectly interpreted if their impact is a multiple greater than the next-highest or next-lowest experiment. The dynamic Y-axis will compress all other bars, making them appear insignificant even when they represent meaningful impact. Mitigation: (1) Add numerical labels on each bar so exact values are visible regardless of visual scale; (2) Investigate log-scale or axis-break options; (3) Include impact values in the table as the authoritative reference for precise numbers.

Risk 2: Deduplication Logic May Miss Edge Cases

Likelihood: Low · Impact: Medium. The deduplication rule (same Feature name, Progression name, Product → keep highest treatment count) may not cover all edge cases — e.g., experiments with slightly different Feature name spellings, or experiments that were stopped and restarted with a new step. Mitigation: (1) Validate deduplication logic against historical data before launch; (2) Add a "Show all variants" toggle for advanced users who want to see the full, unfiltered list.

Risk 3: Data Freshness and Pipeline Latency

Likelihood: Low · Impact: Low. If the underlying scorecard data pipeline has delays, the Metric Impact page may show stale results. Users may not realize the data is not real-time. Mitigation: (1) Display a "Data last updated: [timestamp]" indicator on the page; (2) Document the expected data refresh cadence.
